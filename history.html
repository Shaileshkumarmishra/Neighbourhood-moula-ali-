Qutub Shahi Era (1512 – 1687) :

Golconda/ Qutub Shahi Sultanate – 1512 – 1687

The existence of this area came with the construction of Moula – ali Dargah on a hill during Qutub Shahi dynasty in the year 1578.

Qutub shahi sultans began an annual pilgrimage on 17th Rijab to the hill from Golconda, but after sunni Muslims conquered Hyderabad in 1687, the festival temporarily stopped.

The site emerged as an important religious and sacred site among Shia sect Muslims in and around Hyderabad.

Few studies propose the theory that Hyderabad was named after Hazrat ali Ibn Talib / Moula ali who was regarded as Hyder (lion in Arabic), due to he presence of his shrine in Moula Ali.

Asaf Jhahi Era (1687 – 1950) :

Mughal Dynasty - 1687 – 1724

Asaf jahi Nizam – 1724 – 1950

After the fall of Qutub Shahi dynasty the area came in to the rule of Mughal Dynasty until Hyderabad being declared as a separate state under Asaf Jhah emprorers. Though being Sunni Islam followers Asaf-Jahi dynasties have been a patrons of the shrine and religious and cultural significance.

During Asaf Jahah rule the annual piligrimage again by the Nizams , this festival became one of the two important national festivals of Hyderabad State.

Different Mosques, Madarsas, facilities were developed by the rulers in view of the public visitibg the shrine and settlements around the hills.

By the alliance between Nizam and British established Cantonment (military quarters) in the neighbour and in the Moula ali.

The hill is approximately 614 metres (2,014 ft) tall.

It has 484 steps from dargah to bottom of the hill and has about 600 tombs around it.

There is another hillock near the Moula Ali hill, called "Qadam -e- Rasul", on which the sacred relics of the prophet were deposited by Mohammad Shakrullah Rahan, a servant of Asaf Jahi.

The Moula Ali dargah is located on top of the hill. It was built by Sultan Ibrahim Qutub Shah and Its interior is decorated with thousands of mirrors and it is one of the 11 heritage sites in Hyderabad, identified by the "Heritage conservation committee" of HUDA.
