introduction

moula ali

Moula-Ali commonly known as Moula Ali . It is a well-developed industrial and urban area in Malkajgiri Mandal, of the Medchal-Malkajgiri district, It is a part of Greater Hyderabad and also a part of Hyderabad Metropolitan Region of the Indian state of Telangana, This area is well connected with rail transportation through the Moula Ali Railway Station. It is noted for its Moula Ali hill, on top of which stands the Moula Ali Dargah and mosque, dedicated to Ali.

Infrastructure projects around Moulali

Rail Land Development Authority



is a statutory Authority, under the Ministry of Railways, setup by an Amendment to the Railways Act, 1989, for development of vacant Railway Lands for commercial use for the purpose of generating revenue by non-tariff measures. Feedback Infra (P) Limited (Feedback) has been commissioned to undertake the consultancy study for commercial utilization of a land parcel measuring ~8.99 hectares, located near Moula Ali flyover, Hyderabad.

The site is located in north-eastern part of the city on the 60 ft main road (planned for expansion up to 100 ft as per Master Plan 2031) approaching from Tarnaka to Moula Ali Shrine. Moula Ali Railway Station is located around 1 km away from the site. The site is ~8 km from the Secunderabad CBD (SP road, SD road, RP road etc.) and ~15 km from the Hyderabad CBD (Somajiguda, Banjara Hills, Jubilee Hills). The new international airport is located 38 km away from the site with travel time of ~80-90 minutes. The site is also in close proximity with the proposed Tarnaka Metro station on Blue line of Hyderabad Metro, which is located at a distance of 5 km

The project was proposed by Indian Railways in 2015 setting up goal of completion by 2031. The Moula Ali Flyover Railway Land Development project is a Non-NIP Central PSU Inland Waterways project in Telangana. The project is pending due to land acquisition. There are houses that need to be demolished for road development, and there are also registered lands for which high compensation amounts need to be paid. The Rail Land Development Authority (RLDA) aims to earn Rs 1904 crore from leasing out 16 railway land parcels, including the Moula Ali Flyover in Secunderabad. These sites have been chosen due to their marketability and exclusive locations which make them eminently suitable for commercial and residential development.

Future Potential Moulali

Moula Ali has successfully reached its pinnacle of expansion. The land market in this area has now achieved a stabilized status, demonstrating very minimal fluctuations projected for the future. With the district having utilized its available land to its fullest potential, further development is limited, resulting in a real estate market that promises steadiness and little scope for significant future growth. The area stands as a testament to complete development, showcasing a landscape where expansion has been maximized, leading to a market that boasts stability and predictability in real estate values.

Zoning

Variance in Zoning Factors: If you're referring to how variances interact with zoning factors, a variance is essentially an exception or relaxation of zoning regulations for a specific property or situation. Zoning factors typically include aspects like land use, setbacks, building height, lot coverage, and other parameters that govern how land and buildings can be used and developed within a zoning district. A variance allows a property owner to deviate from these regulations due to unique circumstances or hardships.
by special permits : Special use permits, also known as conditional use permits or special exceptions, are zoning tools used by local governments to regulate land use in specific areas. They allow certain land uses or developments that may not be permitted by right in a particular zoning district but are deemed acceptable under certain conditions. accessory structures: These structures are often regulated by zoning ordinances and codes to ensure they meet certain standards and do not adversely affect the surrounding area.

Streets: Streets are thoroughfares that allow for the passage of vehicles, pedestrians, and sometimes cyclists. They are typically paved and maintained by local municipalities or relevant authorities.

Dead End Streets: A dead end street, also known as a cul-de-sac, is a type of street that does not connect to another street at one end. Instead, it terminates, often forming a circular or semi-circular shape.
